# Grimm Wesen Compendium  
A complete three‑part markdown archive of **all major, minor, hybrid, royal, and supernatural (non‑Wesen) beings** from the TV series **Grimm**.

---

# Part 1 — Major & Notable Wesen  
These Wesen appear repeatedly, affect major plotlines, or are iconic to the series.

## A
### **Adalind’s Hexenbiest / Zauberbiest**
Corpse-like witch Wesen with powerful magic, curses, telekinesis, and spellcasting. Females = Hexenbiest; males = Zauberbiest.

### **Anubis**
Jackal-headed Egyptian Wesen connected to ancient burial rites.

### **Aswang**
Ghoul-like Filipino Wesen that drains infants’ liver fluid with a long tongue.

## B
### **Balam**
Jaguar-like predatory Wesen with stealth and agility.

### **Bauerschwein**
Pig-like Wesen; natural rivals of Blutbaden; frequently farmers or chefs.

### **Blutbad**
Wolf-like Wesen; traditionally violent but can be reformed. Monroe is the most famous.

### **Bullwark**
Rhinoceros-like Wesen known for brute force.

### **Bubba / Cracher-Mortel**
Toad-like Wesen capable of spraying paralytic poison.

## C
### **Canidae (Coyotl)**
Coyote Wesen; strong family traditions and rites of passage.

### **Cerastes**
Horned, intimidating desert predator Wesen.

### **Cracher-Mortel**
See Bubba — poison‑spitting toad Wesen.

## D
### **Dämonfeuer**
Dragon-like fire-generating Wesen.

### **Doyle / Mauvais Dentes**
Sabertooth cat assassin Wesen used by the Royals.

# E–H

## E
### **Eisbiber**
Beaver-like, friendly, nervous community-based Wesen.

### **El Cuegle**
Three-eyed Wesen who foresee future monstrous acts committed by infants and intervene morally.

### **Erdnuckel**
Mole-like Wesen who dig tunnels and often hide underground.

## F
### **Fuchsbau**
Fox Wesen, clever and persuasive; often involved in business or cons.

### **Furis Rubian**
Ram-horned Wesen involved in violent traditions.

## G
### **Gedächtnis Esser**
Octopus-like memory-stealing Wesen used by Royal families.

### **Genio Innocuo**
Turtle-like, gentle scholar Wesen.

### **Gluhenvolk**
Firefly-like Wesen that glow and risk spontaneous combustion.

## H
### **Hässelkopf**
Ram-like Wesen known for powerful headbutts.

### **Hasenpfeffer**
Rabbit-like Wesen; quick, agile, anxious.

### **Hasslich**
Troll-like Wesen, often black-market dealers or criminals.

### **Hexenbiest / Zauberbiest**
Witch-wesen with necromantic and telekinetic powers.

### **Höllentier**
Hellhound-like Wesen with superheated skin.

### **Hundjäger**
Hyena-like bounty hunters.

# Part 3 — Hybrids, Royals & Non-Wesen Supernatural Beings

## Hybrids
### **Hexenbiest × Human (Adalind pre-serum)**
Hybrid retains magic and glamour abilities.

### **Hexenbiest × Zauberbiest Offspring**
Powerful magical bloodlines (e.g., Diana).

### **Wesen × Wesen hybrids**
Occasionally produced; traits vary (mostly rare).

### **Grimm × Wesen (Trubel’s interactions)**
Grimms are not Wesen but interact genetically in rare events.

---

## Royal Line Families (Not Wesen, but superhuman)
### **Viktor, Renard Family, King Frederick**
Human but possess political power, resources, and influence over Wesen.

---

## Purely Supernatural, Non-Wesen

### **La Llorona**
Ghost-like being tied to Hispanic folklore.

### **Gods/Entities (Occasional References)**
Old World myth beings not classified as Wesen.

### **The Ghost in “Stories We Tell Our Young”**
A parasite-based issue, not truly Wesen.

### **Phantoms, Spirits, Demons (Episode-specific)**
Not part of Wesen taxonomy.
