
Here's a simple, popular **flourless blueberry muffin recipe** using exactly the ingredients you mentioned: peanut butter, blueberries, eggs, and bananas. This is a healthy, gluten-free, naturally sweetened option that relies on mashed bananas for moisture and sweetness, peanut butter for structure and richness, and eggs to bind everything.

### Flourless Peanut Butter Blueberry Banana Muffins
**Makes** about 9–12 muffins (depending on tin size and how full you fill them)  
**Prep time**: 10 minutes  
**Bake time**: 15–22 minutes

#### Ingredients
- 2 ripe bananas (the spottier the better for natural sweetness)
- 1 cup creamy peanut butter (natural or regular; avoid very oily or very dry brands for best texture)
- 2 large eggs
- 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries (if frozen, no need to thaw, but they may bleed a bit more color)

#### Optional add-ins (highly recommended for better rise and flavor)
- ½–1 tsp baking powder (helps them puff up more; many versions include this)
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt or cinnamon

#### Instructions
1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a standard muffin tin with paper liners or grease it well (these can stick a bit without liners).

2. In a large bowl, mash the bananas thoroughly with a fork until smooth and almost puree-like.

3. Add the peanut butter and eggs to the mashed bananas. Mix well until fully combined and smooth. (You can use a whisk, spoon, or even a hand mixer for easier blending.) If using vanilla, salt, cinnamon, or baking powder, stir them in now.

4. Gently fold in the blueberries with a spatula so they don't burst too much. The batter will be thick.

5. Scoop the batter evenly into the muffin cups, filling each about ¾ full.

6. Bake for 15–22 minutes, or until the tops look set, lightly golden, and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out mostly clean (a few moist crumbs are okay).  
   - Shorter time (12–15 min) if using baking powder.  
   - Longer time (18–22 min) for denser, more fudgy texture without baking powder.

7. Let the muffins cool in the tin for 5–10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. They firm up as they cool.

#### Tips
- These muffins are dense and moist (almost like a cross between a muffin and a baked oatmeal bite) rather than super light and fluffy, which is normal for flourless versions.
- Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or refrigerate for up to a week. They freeze well too—great for quick breakfasts.
- If the batter seems too thick, a splash of milk (any kind) can help loosen it slightly, but it's usually not needed.
- Fresh blueberries give the best burst of flavor; frozen work fine but might make the muffins a bit wetter.
