
A late-1880s Victorian gothic setting is a great backdrop because it naturally combines industrial progress with ancient superstition. You can have aristocrats hosting séances while steam trains carry hunters into cursed countryside, and scientists compete with occultists to explain the supernatural.

A distinctive feature of a Victorian fantasy setting is the tension between old and new. Gas lamps push back the darkness but cannot banish it. Steam engines race across the countryside while ancient forests still harbor monsters. Universities publish treatises on anatomy even as secret societies preserve medieval grimoires. That collision of industrial modernity, rigid social hierarchy, and ancient supernatural threats gives gothic horror its unique atmosphere.
