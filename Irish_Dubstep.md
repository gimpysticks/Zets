
The Green Lads are a French duo (expanded live) known for blending traditional **Irish/Celtic folk** (violins, reels, uilleann pipes) with modern **electronic dance** elements, creating upbeat, party-friendly "electro-Celtic" or "Irish dubstep" vibes. Their sound mixes energetic folk melodies with EDM drops and rhythms.

Here are some artists with similar fusions of Celtic/folk traditions and electronic, dance, or modern production:

- **The Sidh** — High-energy Celtic folk with electronic beats and powerful violin-driven tracks; often cited as a close match for their festival-ready sound.
- **Aroze** (or Traf Aroze) — Shares the electro-Celtic fusion with catchy, danceable arrangements.
- **Tartalo Music** — Epic Celtic-inspired music with modern production and rhythmic drive.
- **Ian Fontova** — Blends folk elements with accessible, upbeat electronic or cinematic touches.
- **Logan Epic Canto** — Atmospheric Celtic and epic folk with electronic enhancements for an immersive feel.
- **Celkilt** — Punk-influenced Celtic rock with high energy and traditional roots.
- **Beltaine** — Folk with dance and world music elements, keeping things lively and percussive.
- **SYR** — Modern Celtic sounds with electronic and instrumental flair.
- **Aaron Dolan** — Pipe and whistle-driven Celtic music with contemporary twists.
- **The Gothard Sisters** — Folk/Celtic with energetic, melodic, and sometimes produced arrangements.

Other nearby recommendations from music maps and playlists include **Plantec**, **Kinnaris Quintet**, **Rura**, and **Fleuves** for more trad-meets-modern Celtic vibes, or **Snaarmaarwaar** for experimental folk-electronic crossovers.

If you enjoy the "Irish dubstep" side, check live sets or tracks like Green Lads' own "Irish Dubstep" for that specific high-BPM energy, and explore similar festival acts in the electro-folk scene.
