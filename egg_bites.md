Cooking scrambled eggs in cupcake liners or a greased cupcake pan is a great way to meal prep "egg bites." Since air fryers circulate heat quickly, they cook much faster than a traditional oven.

### **Cooking Guide**

| Temperature | Time | Texture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **300°F (150°C)** | **8–10 minutes** | Creamy and tender |
| **325°F (165°C)** | **6–8 minutes** | Firm and slightly browned |

---

### **Quick Tips for Success**

* **Don't Overfill:** Fill the cupcake wells about **2/3 full**. The eggs will puff up significantly (like a souffle) while cooking before settling back down.
* **Prep the Pan:** Even if using a non-stick pan, a quick spritz of oil or using silicone liners helps them pop out easily.
* **Add-ins:** If you add heavy ingredients like sausage, goetta, or chopped peppers, you may need to add **1–2 minutes** to the timer.
* **The "Carry-over" Cook:** Take them out when they look just barely set in the center. The residual heat will finish the job without making them rubbery.

### **Specific Tips for Add-ins**

* **Frozen Vegetables:** Frozen peppers, onions, or spinach release water as they thaw. To prevent "soggy" egg bites, it helps to quickly microwave the veggies for 30 seconds and pat them dry with a paper towel before stirring them into the egg mixture.
* **Cheese Strategy:** Stir half the cheese into the eggs and sprinkle the remaining half on top during the **last 3 minutes** of cooking. This prevents the cheese on top from burning before the center of the egg is set.
* **Denser Meats:** If you are adding something like crumbled sausage or chopped goetta, ensure they are already fully cooked before they go into the egg bites. The air fryer time for eggs isn't long enough to safely cook raw meat from scratch.

### **The "Toothpick" Test**

Because add-ins change the density, the best way to tell they are done is to insert a toothpick or thin knife into the center of a "cupcake." If it comes out clean (no liquid egg), they are ready to be pulled out.

Since you are starting with **frozen sausage patties** at the bottom, the challenge is ensuring the meat heats through completely without overcooking the eggs on top.

Because the frozen sausage acts like a "heat sink," it will take longer for the bottom of the egg to set. I recommend a **two-stage cooking process** or a slightly lower temperature to ensure the sausage is hot and the eggs stay tender.

### **Recommended Cooking Method**

1. **Pre-heat the Sausage (Optional but Recommended):** Place the frozen patties in the cupcake molds and air fry them at **350°F** for **3–4 minutes** first. This thaws the meat and starts the rendering process so the eggs don't sit in a pool of water.
2. **Add the Eggs:** Pour your scrambled egg mixture over the warmed patties, filling the molds about **3/4 full**.
3. **The Main Cook:** Lower the temperature and cook at **300°F** for **12–15 minutes**.

### **Pro-Tips for Frozen Patties**

* **Drain the Liquid:** Frozen sausages often release moisture as they thaw. If you notice liquid in the bottom of the mold after the first few minutes, you can carefully dab it with a paper towel before adding the eggs to keep the texture firm.
* **Center Check:** Since the sausage is frozen, the very center of the patty is the last part to heat up. Use a knife to poke through the egg into the sausage; it should feel hot to the touch, not just warm.
* **The "Puff" Factor:** The eggs will likely puff up quite high because of the steam from the frozen meat. Don't worry—they will settle once you take them out.
