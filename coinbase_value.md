Determining the cash value of your cryptocurrency on Coinbase depends on whether you just want a quick look at its current estimated market value, or if you want to see exactly how much cash you would walk away with after selling it.

Here is how to check both, depending on how you access your account:

### 1. View Estimated Portfolio Value (Quick Glance)

This tells you what your crypto is worth right now based on real-time market prices, before any transaction fees.

* **On the Mobile App:** Tap the **Home** or **My Assets** tab. Your total portfolio value is displayed at the very top in your local currency. To see a breakdown of a specific cryptocurrency, scroll down to your asset list and tap on the individual coin (e.g., Bitcoin or Ethereum).
* **On a Web Browser:** Log in and look at your **Home** dashboard. Your total balance is prominently displayed at the top. You can click on the **My Assets** tab in the left-hand sidebar to see the exact dollar value breakdown for each coin you hold.

---

### 2. Check the Actual Settlement Value (If You Were to Sell)

Because cryptocurrency prices fluctuate and Coinbase charges transaction fees/spreads to convert crypto into fiat currency (like USD), the actual cash you receive will be slightly different than the estimated portfolio value.

To see the exact cash value you can realize:

1. Navigate to the **Buy/Sell** or **Trade** section.
2. Select **Sell**.
3. Choose the specific crypto asset you want to value, and set the destination to your **Cash/USD Balance**.
4. Select **Max** (or type in the full amount of tokens you own).
5. Click **Preview Sell**.

> ⚠️ **Note:** Clicking "Preview" will **not** execute the trade. It safely takes you to a summary screen that shows the exact real-time market rate, subtracts the Coinbase transaction fee, and displays the final **"Total Payout"**—which is the precise cash value that would hit your account.

---

### 3. A Note on "Available Balance" vs. "Total Balance"

If you recently deposited funds from a bank account to buy crypto, you might notice a difference between your total portfolio value and what you are allowed to withdraw.

* **Total Balance:** The current market value of all your holdings.
* **Available Balance:** The amount of cash or crypto you can instantly withdraw or transfer *off* the platform. If you have a temporary bank hold on recent deposits, you can still trade or sell that crypto within Coinbase, but you won't be able to cash out those specific funds to your bank until the hold clears (usually a few business days).
