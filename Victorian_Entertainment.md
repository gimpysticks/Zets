The Music Hall was a **cultural phenomenon** that served as the primary mass entertainment for the working and lower-middle classes in Victorian Britain.

* **Affordable Entertainment:** Music Halls evolved from the "singing saloons" and tavern entertainment of the 1830s. They provided **affordable** and relaxed amusement, contrasting sharply with the formality of traditional theatre and opera.
* **Purpose-Built Theatres:** By the 1850s, the first purpose-built Music Halls appeared (like the famed **Canterbury Hall**). They were designed to allow the audience to **eat, drink, and smoke** at tables in the auditorium while watching the show.
* **Rowdy and Interactive:** The atmosphere was famously **boisterous and convivial**. Audiences were expected to join in the catchy choruses, cheer their favorites, and sometimes even boo or throw things at unpopular acts.
